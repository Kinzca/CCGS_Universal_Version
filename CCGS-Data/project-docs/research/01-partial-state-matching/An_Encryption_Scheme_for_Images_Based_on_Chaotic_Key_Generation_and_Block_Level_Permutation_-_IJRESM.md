Abstract: Privacy of the image data is the major concern in the 

digital world. For this, a new plain image-related and image 

encryption on chaotic scheme is proposed, which includes two 

rounds of encryption operations. The (RNS) Residue number 

system based key generation and shuffling, performed in the initial 

encryption process and is associated the original image 

information with a key, from that the encryption method can 

resists the attacks. The second level repetitive code can adopt in 

the sequence with original parameters, from that against noise and 

data loss attack. The RNS based key generation and encryption 

scheme is experimented with sufficient samples from standard 

database and the performance of the system is evaluated using 

standard measures, show that the scheme not only possesses good 

randomness but also has a high degree of robustness against text, 

data and noise attack. 

Keywords: Image encryption, Decryption, Data loss attack.

2. Proposed system 

A. Chaos based encryption 

To enhance the ability of resisting plaintext attacks, we 

propose a method to extract the plaintext information and 

associate this information with the secret key. Our method 

contains four steps. The initial step is to use the chaotic 

generated sequence method by the first secret key to scramble 

the plain image pixels. After the scrambling operation, we 

divide each pixel into different blocks and set the number of 

blocks equal to the bit width of a pixel. Next, parity checking is 

performed for each block. The last step is to use all the parity 

bits to compose an offset of initial state values in the secret key, 

and the modified key is used for diffusion at the first round. 

Thus, if one bit of plaintext is changed, the corresponding secret 

key will be different. In addition, to protect the plaintext-related 

parameter, the parameter is duplicated for multiple times and 

inserted it into the end of the ciphertext obtained by the first 

encryption process, and then encrypted in the second encryption 

process. The highlevel diagram and encyption diagram is 

shown in Fig. 1 and 2, which comprises of three phases: chaotic 

sequence generation, The first-round encryption process and 

the second-round encryption process. For chaotic sequence 

generation, in order to associate the plaintext with the key and 

enhance the decryption system's defensive ability resist data 

loss attacks and noise attacks,The two chaotic sequence 

generators, Except for the initial values, we apply two identical 

keys to these two chaotic sequence generators. The initial state 

values of one generator are related to secret key only, while 

those of the other generator are related to plaintext information. 

The round one encryption process contains scrambling and 

diffusing operations. Scrambling and diffusion are genrated by 

genrator 1 and genrator 2 respectively that are used by chaotic 

sequence. Other hand, in order to enhance the flexibility of the 

encryption system, it is sufficient to transmit the plaintext 

related parameter separately. So, insert the plaintext related 

parameter at last of the ciphertext obtained in the first 

encryption process. In addition, to protect it in noisy channels, 

we duplicate it multiple times before insertion. This will lead to 

the inhomogeneous distribution of ciphertext histogram, and 

the plaintextrelated parameters will be exposeds easily. 

Then, it requires the second-round encryption. The round two 

encryption process includes the repetitive coding, scrambling 

and diffusion operations. After inserting repetitive codes, the 

additional scrambling- diffusion process maintains a uniform 

distribution of the ciphertext's histogram, and hides the 

plaintext-related information in the final ciphertext. It enhances 

the encryption systems security.

D. Pixel scrambling 

Scan the image to be scrambled progressively in permutation 

process and convert the image pixel matrix into a 1D sequence, 

which has a length of l. Iterate chaotic sequence generator i for 

l times to obtain the needed chaotic sequence S with the length 

of l. Then, quantify its values from 0 to l -1 through S= S mod 

(l), where l is l1 or l2. Since S is not strict uniformly distributed 

in the range of [0, l - 1], S cannot be directly used to scramble 

the pixels. Therefore, we sort it in ascending order, and use the 

indexed values to compose the scrambling vector. If there are 

values in S are the same, the index values of I are still in the 

sequence of original values. Scramble the plain image sequence 

PS to getting of the scrambled sequence D.  

In this scheme, perform two rounds of scrambling process, 

both of which have the same steps. Chaotic sequence generator 

1 iterates for n times to generate the sequence S1, which is used 

to perform the first-round scrambling operations, and obtain the 

scrambled sequence as D1. Chaotic sequence generator 2 

iterates for l2 times to generate the sequence S2, which is used
