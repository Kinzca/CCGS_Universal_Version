# Breaking Down Monument Valley's Visual Style

Image from [design-milk.com](http://1.design-milk.com/images/2014/04/caledonia.jpg)

Colour Palette: Monument Valley utilises a soft, pastel colour palette. This gives the game a natural, serene look. The game contrasts steely greys and deep, mysterious blues with warm, earthy greens and browns.

3D Style: The game uses a minimalist, low-poly style with sharp edges, accentuating the simple complexity of its geometric structures. Architecture is the main visual component of the game, and a low-detail style ensures that the geometry of the structures themselves isn't lost in the whole image.

Lighting: Lighting is limited to simple shading, with ambient occlusion creating shadows in corners and alcoves.

Image from [gedblog.com](http://gedblog.com/wp-content/uploads/monumentvalley_totem_full.jpg)

Fog effects give extra depth to the scene and lift the walkable/interactive structures away from background elements.

The lack of UI/HUD elements on the screen gives the game a clean simplicity which matches its intuitive and simple, yet thought-provoking mechanics.

These are the aspects of Monument Valley's visual style that I'd like to emulate in the final outcome of my project, albeit from a fundamentally different player perspective.

I feel that the refined simplicity of this style will allow the focus of my research project – the visual illusions integrated into the environment – to be showcased effectively.
